# Confluence Bridges

- Simulation ID: `confluence-bridges`
- One-sentence idea: Weighted discipline hubs build and stretch circle bridges as the pointer moves through the field.
- Why it differs: The existing daily rotation has swarms, point clouds, mosaics, and growth systems; this candidate is more about relationships between clusters than a single material body.
- Constraint fit: The visual language stays circle-first, avoids helper lines, and makes connection visible through repeated dots and spacing rather than stroked diagrams.
- Interaction model: Pointer movement attracts and stretches the bridge field; drag should intensify the bridge tension without turning the route into a drawing tool; touch should mirror pointer pressure with a single contact.
- Why candidate level: It is conceptually clear but still needs review for whether the bridge metaphor feels like site material or like an explanatory chart.
- Promotion bar: It becomes promotable if the circle bridges read instantly, perform smoothly on mobile, and add a distinct daily rhythm beside the existing flock, mosaic, and point-cloud modes.
- Deletion bar: Delete it if the bridges feel diagrammatic, if the interaction relies on visible guide lines, or if it does not hold up at small viewport sizes.
