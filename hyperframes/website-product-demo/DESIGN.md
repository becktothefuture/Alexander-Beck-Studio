# Alexander Beck Studio Product Demo

## Style Prompt
Use the Alexander Beck Studio website as the visual source: a quiet dark browser surround, a soft off-white inner wall, tactile physics objects, compact product language, and restrained motion that preserves context. The demo should feel like a precise product walkthrough, not a launch trailer. Screenshots of the live site carry the main visual story; overlays should be minimal, typographic, and tied to the site chrome.

## Colors
- Browser surround: `#181818`
- Studio wall: `#efefef`
- Primary dark text: `#161616`
- Primary light text: `#f0f0f0`
- Accent red: `#f03030`
- Teal: `#00695c`
- Blue: `#0d5cb6`
- Orange: `#ffa000`

## Typography
- UI and headlines: `DM Sans`
- Technical labels and metadata: `DM Mono`
- Use heavy DM Sans for demo statements and DM Mono for small timing/category labels.

## Motion
- Medium-energy product walkthrough pacing.
- Use push, focus-pull, and blur crossfade transitions.
- Keep outgoing scenes fully visible until the transition starts.
- Favor small scale, slide, and opacity entrances over decorative effects.

## What NOT to Do
- Do not invent a separate neon, gradient, or SaaS-dashboard palette.
- Do not cover the website screenshots with large marketing cards.
- Do not add analytics, external tracking, or runtime fetches.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
